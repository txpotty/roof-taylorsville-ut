Roofing in Taylorsville, UT FAQs

How often should I have my roof inspected?

It’s generally recommended to have your roof inspected at least once a year, preferably in the spring or fall. Regular inspections can help identify potential problems early and prevent costly repairs. Additionally, you should have your roof inspected after any major storm or significant weather event.

How long does a new roof last?

The lifespan of a new roof depends on the type of material used and the quality of installation. Asphalt shingle roofs typically last 15-25 years, while tile and metal roofs can last 50 years or more. Proper maintenance can also extend the life of your roof.

What are the signs I need a new roof?

Signs that you may need a new roof include frequent leaks, missing or damaged shingles, a sagging roof deck, excessive granule loss, and water stains on interior ceilings or walls. If you notice any of these signs, it’s best to have your roof inspected by a professional to determine the extent of the damage and the best course of action.
